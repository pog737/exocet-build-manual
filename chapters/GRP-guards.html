GRP Guards

Contents / GRP Guards

Your wheels and tires needed to be fitted for this.

Front and rear process is the same except for the Rear being even over the top of the wheel, and the front being tiled rearwards.

The larger side of the guard is the INSIDE.

  1. Add masking tape to the edge of the guard where you will be drilling for protection.
  2. Ensure the guard frames have sufficient clearance to the tyres. (5mm)
  3. Ensure the guard frames are parrallel.
  4. With the wheel in place rest the guard on the tire and line up the position (as above depending fr/rear)
  5. Mark the masking tape where the center of the guard frames are.
  6. Check you have marked the right side.
  8. Measure distances from holes to outer edge to verify angle.
  9. Drill a 12mm hole slightly higher on the guard; allows the wheel to be 'contained' but not too low or you cant remove the wheel!
  10. Elongate the hole with a round file to almost 24mm high, do this in small steps checking the fit as you go.
  11. When theres a good fit, tape the guard in place and test fit the wheel.
  12. Note the spacing between the mounts and the guard, preferably find some offcut to use as 'packing' this gap.
  13. Using the PU adhesive attach the guard in place.
