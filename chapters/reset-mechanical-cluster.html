Resetting Mechanical Cluster

Contents / Resetting Mechanical Cluster

Much care is required here. any scratch, dust particle or finger print will be highlighted in sunlight later.

  1. Remove the front of the cluster by applying pressure to the 10(?) clips around the edge.
  2. Carefully slide the cover off, note, if you have a heated rear screen (2 wires out the back of the cluster), you will have to feed these through
  3. Remove the 2 large screws next to the main drive
  4. Remove the 2 smaller screws towards the center
  5. Remove the gauge
  6. Note where the needle sits - this will vary depending on the angle you hold the gauge so note this too.
  7. Using 2 small screw drivers leaver the needle off using the screws as pivot points.
  8. Note the angle that the display numbers of the rings sit on.
  9. Remove the 2 screws holding the face on.
  10. On the end of the main odometer, remove the tiny C clip. dont let the rings slip down.
  11. Carefully, lifting only as far as you need, starting from the small number lift and turn rings one at a time.
  12. Make sure that all the rings are sitting in the right place; applying gentle rotational pressure they shouldnt move.
  13. Re fit C clip using a magnetized screw driver to hold and another to push.
  14. Re-assemble the reverse of above.
